Common Questions

FAQ

Do I need a science degree to break into regulated industries?

No. Regulatory Affairs, Quality Assurance, and Regulatory Operations roles hire from a wide range of backgrounds including communications, business, English, and information management. The key is understanding the regulatory language, the frameworks, and how to position your existing experience correctly. That is exactly what The Regulated Path teaches.

Where do I start if I have no industry experience at all?

Start with the free Regulated Industry Primer. It maps the five career tracks, shows you what each pays, and gives you the keywords and certifications to focus on. From there, The Industry Handbook gives you the resume templates and job search strategy to start applying with confidence.

What is the difference between The Complete Course and The Cohort?

The Complete Course is self-paced with lifetime access. You work through eight modules on your own schedule and get The Pathway Practicum bundled in. The Career Launch Cohort adds live instruction, peer accountability, resume reviews, and mock interviews over six structured weeks. Both include the full curriculum. The Cohort is for professionals who want direct feedback and a group environment.

How long does it take to land a role after going through the program?

It depends on your starting point, how aggressively you apply, and the market at the time. Most professionals working through the materials consistently see traction within 60 to 90 days of focused application activity. The 90-Day Action Plan in the Primer is built around a realistic timeline for a first regulated industry role.

Is Executive Coaching right for me?

Executive Coaching is for professionals who are serious about moving fast on a specific goal and want a senior practitioner directly in their corner. It is not for everyone. If you are still in the orientation phase, start with the Primer or the Handbook. If you have a clear target role, an active job search, and want personalized strategy, apply for coaching.
