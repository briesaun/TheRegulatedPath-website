Common Questions

FAQ

Do I need a science degree to break into regulated industries?

No. Regulatory Affairs, Quality Assurance, and Regulatory Operations roles hire from a wide range of backgrounds including communications, business, English, and information management. The key is understanding the regulatory language, the frameworks, and how to position your existing experience correctly. The Regulated Path teaches that for entrants, career-changers, and professionals already inside the industry who want to deepen their expertise.

Where do I start if I have no industry experience at all?

Start with the free PDF in the hero section. It maps the five career tracks, shows you what each pays, and gives you the keywords and certifications to focus on. From there, The Industry Handbook gives you the resume templates and job search strategy to start applying with confidence.

I am already in regulated industry. Can The Regulated Path help me move up?

Yes. The Complete Course includes 3,000+ scenario questions across six specialized tracks designed to deepen career knowledge at any stage, plus annotated submission templates and industry intelligence that go beyond entry-level material. Executive Coaching is built for professionals at any level who want direct strategic support on a specific career move.

What is the difference between The Complete Course and The Cohort?

The Complete Course is on-demand with lifetime access. You work through eight modules on your own schedule and get the full library of scenario questions, submission templates, and industry intelligence. The Career Launch Cohort wraps the same Course content in a six-week live group experience with weekly sessions, written feedback on your resume and LinkedIn, mock interviews, salary negotiation drills, and peer accountability. The Cohort is for people who want structure and a group environment. The Course is for people who want to move at their own pace.

What is the difference between The Cohort and Executive Coaching?

The Cohort is a group program centered on the Course curriculum. Executive Coaching is a separate track with no curriculum and no teaching, focused entirely on personalized 1:1 strategic work, including positioning, LinkedIn content strategy, live offer negotiation support, and warm network introductions. Pick the Cohort if you want structured education and group accountability. Pick Coaching if you have a specific career move in mind and want a senior practitioner directly in your corner.

How long does it take to land a role after going through the program?

It depends on your starting point, how aggressively you apply, and the market at the time. Most professionals working through the materials consistently see traction within 60 to 90 days of focused application activity. The 90-Day Action Plan in the free PDF is built around a realistic timeline for a first regulated industry role.

Is Executive Coaching right for me?

Executive Coaching is for professionals at any career stage who are serious about moving fast on a specific goal and want a senior practitioner directly in their corner. It is not for everyone. If you are still in the orientation phase, start with the free PDF or the Handbook. If you have a clear target role, an active job search, or a specific career move you are working toward, apply for coaching.
