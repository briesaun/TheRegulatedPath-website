Curriculum

Eight modules. 40+ hours of curriculum.

Self-paced, downloadable workbooks per module, quarterly content updates as the regulatory landscape shifts.

Module 01

Foundations of Regulated Industries

The architecture of how medical devices, pharmaceuticals, and biologics are regulated globally. Vocabulary, agencies, and core concepts.

Module 02

Global Regulatory Landscape

FDA, EMA, MHRA, PMDA, MDSAP, ANVISA, NMPA. How they relate, where they diverge, and how submissions flow across markets.

Module 03

Quality Management Systems and Standards

ISO 13485, 21 CFR Part 820, MDSAP, ICH Q-series. How quality systems actually operate inside regulated companies.

Module 04

Premarket Pathways and Submissions

510(k), De Novo, PMA, IND, NDA, BLA, MDR Technical File. What goes in each, who reviews it, and how decisions are made.

Module 05

Clinical and Non-Clinical Evidence

Evidence requirements, study design fundamentals, biocompatibility, sterilization, and clinical evaluation reports.

Module 06

Post-Market Surveillance and Lifecycle Management

Vigilance, MDR reporting, PMS plans, PMCF, change control, and the realities of managing a product after approval.

Module 07

Career Architecture and Launch

Targeting roles, building your portfolio, networking inside the industry, interview strategy, and offer negotiation.

Module 08

Operating in the Role

The first 90 days in a regulatory role. How to ramp, who to align with, and how to get a strong first review.
